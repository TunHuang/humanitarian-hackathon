Scholar Farmers is a non profit organisation that was formed in 2018 after we came to a realization that there was an immense lack of farming knowledge in our children.We’re currently operating in 5 countries supporting 405 schools in farming projects,this initiative started in south African,continued in Namibia and ultimately spreaded in countries like Greece,Italy and Taiwan via the help of our partners UN Food and Greek Agricultural government.

Agriculture is one of the oldest occupations of mankind. With industrialization taking over our lives including that of our kids, the focus has shifted from what is good to what is the trend. We are changing the narrative, taking it back to where it all began...NATURE. We offer educational agricultural material in primary schools. Our goal is to get schools around the world to start teaching farming as a compulsory subject. We invite the world to join and rally behind this initiative in promoting environmental health.As per the research done by food and agriculture organisation, the youth is acutely impacted by climate change, we can change this,it is in our hands.

Our Approach
Access to real farmers

  • We have a substantial number of farmer visits who are willing to voluntarily take time off to come to our little farms, they assist with practical work.
  • We also have speakers who come to our schools and do motivational talks

Educational resources

  • Books
  • Documentaries (film)
  • Tools - all tools are demonstrated to ensure safely use
  • First aid lessons

Our Values

Children are our future

The great Nelson Mandela once said -”history will judge us by the difference we make in the lives of our children”. It is up to to us to help them make better health choices for their future.

Respect and Nurture

Teaching our kids about an in depth understanding of the relationship between nature and humans will make them nurture and respect our environment.

Farming is Cool

We’re changing the mindset of the children to grow up understanding the coolness that is behind farming. Instilling this at such a tender age could only normalise the subject of farming and ultimately change the children’s mindset.
